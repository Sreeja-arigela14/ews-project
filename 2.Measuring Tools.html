2. Measuring Tools

A. Steel Rule

These are made up of stainless steel and are available in many sizes ranging from 1/2 ft. to 2 ft. These are marked in inches or millimetres. All the faces are machined true. The edges of steel rule should be protected from rough handling.

B. Calipers

These are generally used to measure the inside or outside diameters. Different types are:

  • Outside Callipers: It is used to measure the outside dimensions.
  • Inside Callipers: It is used to measure the inside dimensions.
  • Spring Callipers: Spring is provided to apply the pressure and lock nut is provided to lock any desired position.
  • Hermaphrodite, Jenny or Oddleg Callipers: One leg is bent at the tip inwardly and the other has a straight pointed end. It is used to scribe lines parallel to the straight edges.

C. Vernier Calliper

It is used for measuring the outer dimensions of round, flat, square components and also the inner size of the holes and bore. A narrow blade is used to measure the depth of bar slots etc. The reading accuracy in metric system is 0.02 mm and in the British system, it is 0.001". It is made of stainless steel.
